## Why Not TF-IDF / Embeddings / RAG

TF-IDF finds what is distinctive per document -- topic words. The cognitive fingerprint lives in the OPPOSITE place: words common across ALL documents. "Because," "therefore," "actually," "honestly." How you reason. TF-IDF removes these. The fish centers on them.

RAG retrieves what was said. The fish knows what was meant.

Embeddings measure semantic similarity. The fish measures cognitive mode.

Topic map vs cognition map. d=2.245 is the measured gap. Published. Replicated. N=46.

## Topic Map vs Cognition Map

Every other approach builds a topic map. TF-IDF finds distinctive words. Embeddings find semantic similarity. RAG retrieves relevant passages. All of these answer: what did the user talk about?

The fish builds a cognition map. It finds the words that are common across ALL of a person's writing -- the positional cognitive words that reveal how they reason, connect, hedge, and decide. "Because," "therefore," "actually," "honestly," "look," "think," "feel." TF-IDF removes these as noise. The fish treats them as the primary signal.

A person who writes "actually, I think the real issue is..." in emails about parenting AND emails about architecture has a cognitive pattern: they reframe before they assert. That pattern is invisible to topic-based analysis. The fish sees it immediately because it measures cognitive mode, not subject matter.

The flat field is the basin. Volume is basin not noise. Formations rise from it when the vocabulary creates topography. Without cognitive vocabulary, the landscape is a salt flat -- everything equidistant. With it, mountains appear.
