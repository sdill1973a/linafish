### Your First Feeding

```

linafish go ~/my-writing
```

Point it at anything you've written. Journals. Emails. Notes. That novel draft. The fish doesn't judge. It eats.

After its first meal, check the portrait:

That's a **formation**. Your fish noticed a pattern in how you think. You didn't ask it to. It found it because the math found it. More on that in Part III.

### How Often to Feed

Your fish is not needy. It will eat whenever you offer food. Some suggestions:

| Frequency | What to feed | Why |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Daily | Journal entries, notes | The fish tracks how you change over time |

### What Not to Feed

Your fish will eat anything, but some food is better than others:

- **Don't feed it other people's writing** (unless you want a portrait of them)
- **Don't feed it code** (unless you want it to analyze your architecture brain)
- **Don't feed it the same thing twice** (it deduplicates, but why waste a meal)
- **Don't worry about grammar** — the fish doesn't care about your spelling

### Signs of a Healthy Fish

- **5-50 formations** — your fish has found real patterns
- **Keywords that sound like you** — not generic, YOUR words
- **Formations that surprise you** — "huh, I do talk about that a lot"
- **The portrait makes your AI better** — conversations feel more like talking to someone who knows you

### Signs Something Is Off

- **1 giant formation** — the fish needs centroid subtraction (see Appendix B)
- **0 formations** — not enough food yet, or gamma is too high
- **100+ formations** — the fish is over-splitting. Feed it more and let it reconsolidate
- **Keywords that don't sound like you** — wrong `d` setting (see Appendix C)

### What Are Formations?

Formations are clusters of related thoughts that the fish found in your writing. Nobody told the fish what to look for. The math found it.

Think of it this way: if you wrote 100 journal entries and a very patient friend read all of them, they might say "you know, you always come back to three things: your kids, your work stress, and that trip to Italy." Those are formations.

Your fish does this automatically. With math. From 1912.

### How the Math Works (the short version)

1. Your writing gets broken into **crystals** — small pieces, each with a unique fingerprint
2. Each crystal gets a **vector** — a list of numbers describing which words appear together in unusual ways
3. Crystals with similar vectors **couple** — they link together
4. Linked clusters become **formations** — named groups with a personality

That's it. No AI model. No GPU. No training data. Just: what words hang out together in YOUR writing, and what clusters form when we look at the whole picture.

### The Eight Dimensions

Your fish sees your writing through eight lenses:

| Code | Question | What it detects |
|------|----------|----------------|
| KO | What do you **know**? | How you synthesize and recognize patterns |
| TE | What do you **test**? | How you verify, question, check against truth |
| SF | What do you **structure**? | How you organize and build frameworks |
| CR | What do you **relate to**? | How you connect to people and meaning |
| IC | What do you **want**? | Your intentions, emotions, desires |
| DE | What do you **specialize in**? | Your domain expertise, technical depth |
| EW | What do you **do**? | How you act, build, execute |
| AI | What do you **reflect on**? | How you think about your own thinking |

Every formation gets labeled with its dominant dimensions. `ACTING+RELATING_via_FEELING` means: this cluster of your writing is about doing things that connect to people, driven by emotion.

### Why the Portrait Works

When you paste your fish.md into an AI's instructions, the AI doesn't get a list of facts about you. It gets a map of how you THINK. It knows:

- What topics you keep returning to (formations)
- How you process them (dimensions)
- What your actual words sound like (crystal quotes)
- What patterns you don't see yourself (the surprise factor)

This is why the AI suddenly "gets" you. It's not smarter. It just has your cognitive map.

## APPENDICES

### Appendix A: The d Parameter

The `d` parameter controls how your fish selects its vocabulary.

- **d ≤ 2 (warm):** Frequency is signal. Words you use OFTEN are important. Best for personal writing where your signature words ARE you.
- **d = 3-5 (blend):** Mix of frequency and distinctiveness. Best for general use.
- **d > 5 (stranger):** Rare words matter. Best for diverse corpora where you want the unusual.

### Appendix B: Centroid Subtraction

If your fish has one giant formation, your voice is too consistent — everything sounds like you (because it is). Centroid subtraction removes your baseline signal and exposes the variation within. Like removing the carrier wave to hear the song.

```
FishEngine(subtract_centroid=True)
```

### Appendix C: Gamma Threshold

Gamma controls how easily crystals couple. The fish sets this automatically (adaptive threshold at p75 of sampled pairs). If your formations are too many and too small, gamma is too low. If too few and too large, gamma is too high. Usually you don't need to touch this.
